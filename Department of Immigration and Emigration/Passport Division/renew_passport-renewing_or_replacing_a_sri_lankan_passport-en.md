# Replacing a Damaged Passport

A passport may be considered damaged when important information cannot be read, the photograph or data page is damaged, pages are missing, or the passport has been exposed to water, fire, or chemicals.

# Reporting a Lost or Stolen Passport

**A lost or stolen passport should be reported immediately. **Once a passport has been officially reported and cancelled, it must not be used again, even if it is later found.

## When the Passport Is Lost in Sri Lanka

- Inform the Department of Immigration and Emigration.
- Make a complaint at the nearest police station.
- Provide accurate details about the lost passport.
- Follow the instructions given by the police and the Department.
- Apply for a new passport.
## When the Passport Is Lost Overseas

- Report the loss to the local police in the relevant country.
- Obtain a police report.
- Complete the required lost or stolen passport complaint form.
- Contact the nearest Sri Lankan diplomatic mission.
- Submit the police report and required documents.
- Follow the mission’s instructions for a replacement travel document.
# Can a Reported Passport Be Used Again?

**No. **A passport officially reported as lost or stolen becomes invalid and must not be used for travel.
