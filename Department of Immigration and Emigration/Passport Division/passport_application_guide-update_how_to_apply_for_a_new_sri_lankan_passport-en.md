# How to Apply for a New Sri Lankan Passport

# Overview

**A Sri Lankan passport** is an official travel document issued to eligible Sri Lankan citizens. Applicants must provide accurate personal information and complete the applicable identity-verification process.

# Types of Passport Services

# General Eligibility

- Be a Sri Lankan citizen by descent or registration.
- Provide accurate and complete personal information.
- Submit the required original documents and photocopies.
- Provide a photograph according to the current standards.
- Provide fingerprints when required.
- Attend the relevant office personally when identity verification is necessary.
# Documents Commonly Required

- A completed passport application.
- Original birth certificate and a photocopy.
- National Identity Card and a photocopy.
- Digital photograph acknowledgement.
- Current or previous passport, when applicable.
- Citizenship registration certificate, when applicable.
- Marriage certificate or other supporting documents when requesting changes to personal information.
# Application Process

1. Obtain the application: Visit the Department of Immigration and Emigration or a divisional secretariat to collect Form K. This form is required to initiate your application.
2. Complete the application: Fill in all personal details. Once completed, proceed to the Department of Immigration and Emigration office.
3. Prepare documents: Gather all required original documents and photocopies. Ensure these are ready before arriving at the office for submission.
4. Provide digital photograph: Visit an approved photo studio to obtain your digital photograph acknowledgement. Bring this to the office during your submission.
5. Submit the application: Go to the Department of Immigration and Emigration office. Hand over your documents to the counter officer to begin processing.
6. Complete identity verification: Remain at the office to provide fingerprints and verify your personal information with the officer. You will receive a receipt for your application.
7. Collect the passport: Return to the office on the date specified on your receipt or wait for notification to collect your passport.

# Applications for Children

For passport purposes, a child or minor applicant may require the involvement and consent of parents or a legal guardian. Common supporting documents may include:

- Child’s birth certificate.
- Parents’ passports or National Identity Cards.
- Consent letter from the parents.
- Current passport, when available.
- Adoption or guardianship documents, where applicable.
# Important Instructions

- Check that all personal details are correct before submission.
- Ensure that the name entered matches the supporting documents.
- Do not provide false or misleading information.
- Keep the application receipt safely.
- Check the official Department of Immigration and Emigration website for current requirements.
- Do not make travel arrangements until the passport is issued.
