# Our Winter Reserve Is Here



Every December we release a single limited-lot roast sourced from
a small cooperative of growers in the highlands. This year's Winter
Reserve comes from a farm that has worked with us for six seasons,
and it might be the best batch yet.



## Where It Comes From



The beans are grown at roughly 1,700 meters, hand-picked over three
weeks, and washed within hours of harvest. That short window between
picking and processing is a big part of why the cup tastes as clean
as it does — bright citrus up front, a soft caramel sweetness
underneath, and a finish that lingers a little longer than you'd
expect from a medium roast.



> "We've been buying from this cooperative since our second year in
> business. The Winter Reserve is the one roast our whole team argues
> about — in a good way." — Head Roaster

## How to Brew It



This roast is forgiving across most brew methods, but it really
shines with a pour-over. We recommend:


- 18g of coffee to 300g of water
- Water just off the boil, around 94°C
- A total brew time of 2:45 to 3:15
- A medium-fine grind, similar to coarse sand


A batch this small doesn't last — we roasted just enough for a
four-week window, and once it's gone, it's gone until next winter.
